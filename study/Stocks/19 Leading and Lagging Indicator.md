## üîπ What Are Indicators?

Indicators are **tools derived from price, volume, or economic data** that help traders and analysts **predict or confirm** trends.

They fall broadly into two types:

| Type                   | Description                                         | Use                                                |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Leading Indicators** | Predict future price movements or economic changes. | Used for **early entry** or **anticipation**.      |
| **Lagging Indicators** | Confirm trends or reversals after they have begun.  | Used for **confirmation** and **trend-following**. |

---

## ‚ö°Ô∏è In Technical Analysis (Trading Context)

### üü¢ **Leading Indicators**

These **signal a potential trend or reversal before it happens**.
They are based on **price momentum, volume shifts, or sentiment**.

**Examples:**

| Indicator                             | What It Does                                             | Type        |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **RSI (Relative Strength Index)**     | Measures overbought/oversold; may predict reversal       | Momentum    |
| **Stochastic Oscillator**             | Compares close price to range; signals reversal early    | Momentum    |
| **MACD Histogram** (early divergence) | Shows early change in momentum                           | Momentum    |
| **Volume / OBV (On-Balance Volume)**  | Rise in volume before price move                         | Volume      |
| **Williams %R**                       | Similar to Stochastics                                   | Momentum    |
| **CCI (Commodity Channel Index)**     | Detects cyclical reversals                               | Momentum    |
| **Fibonacci Retracements**            | Predicts potential support/resistance before trend shift | Price Level |
| **Bollinger Band %B or Squeeze**      | Contraction hints at upcoming volatility breakout        | Volatility  |

**Advantages:**

* Gives **early warning** for entry or exit.
* Useful in **range-bound** or **reversal** trading.

**Limitations:**

* **Many false signals.**
* Less reliable in strong trends.

---

### üîµ **Lagging Indicators**

These **react to price movement** and confirm that a trend is underway.
They are **trend-following tools**.

**Examples:**

| Indicator                           | What It Does                                        | Type           |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| **Moving Averages (SMA, EMA)**      | Smooth price data; confirm up/downtrend             | Trend          |
| **MACD Line Crossovers**            | Confirms momentum and trend direction               | Trend          |
| **ADX (Average Directional Index)** | Confirms strength of a trend                        | Trend Strength |
| **Parabolic SAR**                   | Follows trend direction                             | Trend          |
| **Supertrend**                      | Uses ATR; confirms buy/sell only after trend begins | Trend          |
| **Ichimoku Cloud (Lagging Span)**   | Confirms momentum & support/resistance              | Trend          |

**Advantages:**

* **High reliability** once the trend is confirmed.
* Ideal for **trend-following** and **position trading**.

**Limitations:**

* **Late entries and exits.**
* May cause **whipsaws** in ranging markets.

## üìä Combined Use (Best Practice)

Most professional traders **combine leading and lagging indicators** for higher accuracy:

| Goal         | Indicator Type | Example Combo                                      |
| ------------ | -------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Early Entry  | Leading        | RSI < 30 ‚Üí oversold                                |
| Confirmation | Lagging        | EMA10 > EMA20 ‚Üí trend confirmation                 |
| Exit Signal  | Either         | RSI crosses 70 (leading) + EMA crossover (lagging) |

**Example Strategy:**

> * Buy when RSI < 30 (oversold) **and** EMA10 crosses above EMA20.
> * Exit when RSI > 70 **or** EMA10 crosses below EMA20.

This way, the leading indicator (RSI) gives the early hint, and the lagging one (EMA) confirms it.
